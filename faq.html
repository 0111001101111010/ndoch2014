Frequently Asked Questions:

What will we be doing?

Working on challenges, organizing the community, and discussing what we can do to impact change on a local, regional, and national level.

I don't code or design?

You don’t have to be a coder or a designer to participate in National Day of Civic Hacking. Here’s a sample list of other Brigade activities in which you can participate: Draft and publish open data strategy for your city Source challenges by conducting interviews, creating and publishing challenge briefs Being Subject Matter Experts about your community and issues Liberate public data from closed systems

What Should I bring?

  • Computer
  • Tablet or Mobile Device
  • Pen, paper, anything you need to be constructive
  • Friend(s) or Family
  • Camera
  • A civic hacking attitude!

What is Code for Hampton Roads?

We are a Code for America Brigade, which means we're volunteers that contribute our talents toward improving the way our local governments and community organizations use the web. Brigade is about action and you're here because you are someone who takes action.
